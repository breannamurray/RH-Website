About

RowdyHackers is a student organization formed at the University of Texas at San Antonio to encourage and spread hacker culture on campus through various hack events and projects. We are a community of diverse students with a common goal to learn and build new technology.

We aim to host hack nights, tech talks and workshops hosted by tech companies, and RowdyHacks, compete in various hackathons, and many other events. If you would like more information about our organization, feel free to contact us at UTSAhackers@gmail.com!

FAQ

What is "hacking"?


As a student at the nation's top Cyber Security school, you might not have the best opinion about hacking. Unlike illegal hacking you may have seen on movies, we don't mean hacking as in gaining unauthorized access into data in a computer. By hacking, we simply mean building cool things by working on various innovative projects.


Can I join even if I don't know how to code?


Of course! RowdyHackers aims to be a great learning environment for everyone, including beginners. As long as you have a passion for technology and a desire to learn, we have a place for you in the RowdyHackers family.

How do I join?


If you are interested in joining, feel free to shoot us an email at UTSAhackers@gmail.com so that we can get you on our mailing list for updates. We will let you know when we will be having meetings and other events


What is RowdyHacks?


RowdyHacks will be UTSA's first hackathon hosted by RowdyHackers. This event will be similar to Major League Hacking (MLH) events that are hosted nationwide by other universities and is set to take place sometime this fall. More information about RowdyHacks will be posted in the near future so be on the look out!
